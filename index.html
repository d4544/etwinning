About the Project

This is a year-long international eTwinning project (September 2025 – May 2026) designed for secondary school students. The project explores women’s rights, activism, and representation in cinema and society through intercultural collaboration, critical thinking, and creative work.

Similar projects centered on films such as Suffragettes have been conducted over the past two years, with continuous refinement and development. Participants work in international teams to create digital stories, videos, and presentations that bring historical perspectives and film language into focus.

Why eTwinning?

Connecting classrooms across Europe to build confidence, empathy, and real-world skills.

Goals

International teamwork & intercultural collaboration

Deeper understanding of women’s rights through cinema

Critical thinking about activism and representation

Building empathy and historical awareness

What Pupils Are Learning

  • Stronger spoken and written English
  • Digital storytelling and video-making skills
  • Film language, techniques & visual storytelling
  • How to support arguments with facts and examples
  • Digital presentation and group coordination across borders
  • Cultural empathy and a deeper understanding of gender issues
  • Collaborative research and creative problem-solving

Campaign Timeline (Sept 2025 – May 2026)

September – October
Getting to know each other, exploring women’s rights history, watching and studying the movie "Suffragette".
November – January
Analysing films, creating Xmas and New Year's wishes and reenacting the movie "Suffragette".
February – April
Celebrating International Women's Day, video-making, activism campaigns & presentations
May
Final showcase and celebration of S.H.E. Power
